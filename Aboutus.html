Pentecost University (PU) traces its history to the year 1954 when The Church of Pentecost started a World Mission Bible College (WMBC).
In February 1972, WMBC which had not been active for a long time was re-started as Pentecost Bible Centre (PBC) at the McKeown Temple in Kumasi,
with an initial enrollment of five. Rev. David Mills was the first Principal. Rev James McKeown, founder of The Church of Pentecost (COP), and Rev Joseph Egyir-Paintsil,
the General Secretary, performed the opening ceremony. The Centre was closed in 1975, and was reopened at New Abossey Okai, Accra, in 1983.


In 1984, the Pentecost Bible Centre moved from Kumasi to Madina-Accra. Rev. Martinson K. Yeboah was the first Dean of Students for PBC, serving from 1984 to 1986.
Following Rev. M.K Yeboah as Dean of Students were Rev. L. A. Nyarko (1986-1989), Rev. Emmanuel Parker (1989-1992),
Rev. Samuel Opoku-Adipah (1992-1999), Rev. Kwame Blankson (1999-2003) and Rev. Daniel O. Walker (2003-2005 & 2010-2016),
Rev. Joshua Yirenkyi Smart (2005-2010), Rev. Paul Danso (2016-2020) and Rev. Jonathan Kofie (2020- date). The position of Director of Studies, now abolished, was held by Rev. Dr. Amponsah–Kuffour (1992-2003).

The Pentecost University Council, first headed by Elder Dr. Paul Awua, was inducted on May 6, 2004 at the 10th Session of the Extraordinary Council Meeting of the Church of Pentecost held at the University’s Sowutuom campus.
He was succeeded by Her Ladyship Mrs. Elizabeth Ankumah who served from 2012 to 2020. Currently, the Council is under the Chairmanship of Rev. Prof. Otoo Ellis.
